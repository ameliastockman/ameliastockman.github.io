Chapter A5

The Stacks users extend beyond earth. Orbiting above our heads
are an exponentially increasing number of satellites — watching, collecting and communicating infinite array of data. They spy on earth, recording high resolution images and video, modelling and mapping terrain. They monitor weather, atmosphere, climate change, gravitational fields, agriculture, the oceans, urban environments, and all of earths citizens on behalf of governments and private corporations alike. Satellites connect all users of the stack (human and non-human) by means of radio, television, phone and broadband internet, making almost place unreachable.

More than 11,000 satellites have reached orbit since the first was successfully launched into space by Russia in October of 1957. When 2021 began there were 3,372 active satellites orbiting earth and by the end of April — only 119 days later — there were 7,389. Every object in orbit larger than 10cm is assigned 9 digit Satellite Catalog Number, of which there are currently more than 48,000, their location live and accessible to anyone online.
